EmojiChess - Rules

Rules

There are two players: α and β. α is the player to make the first move. Then they alternate in making moves. Two moves are called a full-move. The board is an 8x8 board. The square on the bottom left is labeled A1 and the square on the top right is labeled H8. α starts on ranks 1-2 and β starts on ranks 7-8. The square D5 is on the fifth rank on the D-file.

Both players start with a randomly selected civilization. A player can not have the same civilization as the other player.

A player wins by capturing the opponents Tier 3 piece and a player loses by losing their Tier 3 piece. If a player moves a Tier 0 piece to the other side of the board it is promoted to whatever that player's civilization's Tier 2 piece is. If a player has only their Tier 3 piece left they lose. It is thus possible for a player to win by capturing all the opponent's pieces except the Tier 3 piece. If all the pieces on the board are in the exact same position for the third time then the game ends as a draw. It doesn't matter whose turn it was. If there have been 50 moves without a forward move of a Tier 0 piece or a capture of any piece the game ends in a draw as well. If a player can not make any legal move that player loses the game.

Motivation

The motivation behind all this is pretty much the same as for other chess variants. Chess focuses a great deal on studying openings and lines which is both good and bad. It can sometimes feel a bit repetitive once you've gone for the 420th Ruy Lopez. EmojiChess has 6 (active) civilizations which amounts to 30 combinations... times two for the sides so there's 60 different starting positions. But to still discourage people from just memorizing lines forever each year 6 civilizations from a pool of civilizations are selected at random to be played for that year.

Competition Rules

A competition period is 12 months long. Before each game each player can (but doesn't have to) ban one civilization. Then the players will get asigned a randomly selected civilizations from the remaining 4 civilizations. This is also to account for potential imbalances between civilizations.
